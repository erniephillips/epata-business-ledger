# EPATA Small-Business Tax Guide

Verified against official sources on June 9, 2026. This is planning and recordkeeping guidance, not legal or tax advice. Confirm entity classification, registrations, and filing obligations with a qualified tax professional or the taxing agency.

## Facts the app tracks

- Gross receipts from every reportable sale, whether or not a tax form is received.
- Marketplace, card, digital-transfer, cash, and other payment channels.
- Sales-tax handling: marketplace-remitted, seller-collected, exempt/not taxable, or review.
- Platform/payment fees, shipping-label costs, refunds, and estimated COGS.
- Paid expenses, business purpose, tax category, business-use percentage, and receipt proof.
- Assets, in-service dates, business-use percentage, and tax-treatment review.
- MakerWorld and other non-cash rewards.
- Business mileage, parking, and tolls.
- Federal, New Jersey, sales-tax, annual-report, and other filing/payment obligations.

## Federal

- Self-employed people generally file an annual return and pay estimated tax quarterly.
- Schedule SE generally applies when net self-employment earnings are $400 or more.
- Individuals generally make federal estimated payments when they expect to owe at least $1,000 after withholding and refundable credits.
- Calendar-year estimated-tax due dates are generally April 15, June 15, September 15, and January 15 of the following year.
- Keep records that support income, deductions, credits, and return items.

## New Jersey

- New Jersey estimated Gross Income Tax payments are generally required when estimated tax is more than $400 beyond amounts withheld and credited.
- Estimated-payment due dates are generally April 15, June 15, September 15, and January 15.
- A business registered to collect NJ sales tax must file quarterly ST-50 returns, even for a quarter with no sales or tax due.
- ST-50 quarterly returns are generally due April 20, July 20, October 20, and January 20.
- NJ sales-tax registrants that collected more than $30,000 in sales tax in the prior calendar year must make monthly payments.
- NJ annual reports are due each year in the business entity's formation/authorization month. The current online filing fee shown by NJ is $75.

## Questions to confirm

Complete Tax Setup in the app:

- Is EPATA treated as a single-member LLC/Schedule C, partnership, S corporation, or something else?
- Is EPATA registered to collect NJ sales tax?
- What month was the LLC formed or authorized in NJ?
- Are there employees?
- Are contractors paid for services?
- Is a personal vehicle used for business?
- Is there a qualifying home office?
- How should filament/material inventory and COGS be handled?

## Monthly routine

1. Enter and reconcile all sales.
2. Enter platform/payment fees, shipping-label costs, refunds, and COGS.
3. Enter expenses and attach proof.
4. Log business mileage.
5. Review sales-tax handling for direct and marketplace sales.
6. Open Tax Prep and clear the review queue.
7. Back up the database.
